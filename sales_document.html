Churn Predictor The Problem

Churn is the silent killer of SaaS growth.

It doesn't show up on your dashboard until it's already gone. By the time a customer cancels, it's too late — the decision was made weeks earlier. And most founders are flying completely blind.

What SaaS founders are stuck doing instead

  • ✕ Gut feeling. Guessing which customers are unhappy based on support tickets or anecdotal Slack messages — missing the quiet ones who just stop logging in.
  • ✕ Reactive outreach. Only reaching out after a customer files a cancellation. At that point, win-back rates drop below 15%.
  • ✕ Spreadsheet triage. Sorting customers by MRR and manually eyeballing usage metrics — time-consuming, inaccurate, and doesn't scale past 100 accounts.
  • ✕ Waiting on a data team. Hiring or contracting a data scientist for a churn model costs $15–80k and takes 3–6 months to deliver anything useful.
  • ✕ No action from the model. Even when founders get a churn score, there's no guidance on what to do — just a probability number that requires its own interpretation.
"We knew something was off with our churn numbers, but we had no idea which customers were about to leave or why. By the time we noticed, we'd already lost three enterprise accounts in one quarter."
— Common feedback from early-stage SaaS founders

The opportunity cost is enormous. A SaaS business with 500 customers at $300 MRR and 8% monthly churn is losing ~$144,000 per year to preventable cancellations. Retaining even 30% of those customers covers the tool cost many times over.

Common Questions
What data do I need to get started?
A CSV with customer records — any combination of contract type, tenure, monthly charges, usage metrics, or support history. The platform handles feature engineering automatically. If you can export from Salesforce, HubSpot, or a spreadsheet, you're ready.
How accurate is the churn prediction?
Accuracy depends on your data quality, but typical results land between 88–94% AUC. The system is optimized for recall — it would rather flag a false positive than miss an at-risk customer. You can view full model metrics (AUC, precision, recall, F1) before acting on results.
Do I need to retrain the model every time?
No. Train once, save the model to the cloud, then score new customer batches as often as you like — daily, weekly, or monthly. Retraining is recommended every 60–90 days or when your customer mix changes significantly.
Is my customer data secure?
Data is processed in your session and not stored on our servers. If you use cloud model storage (Supabase), only the trained model parameters are saved — not your raw customer records.
